    Mathematical Model — Spatial SEIR ILM

    Disease progression

    S → E → I → R

    E: Latent (not infectious) + Presymptomatic (infectious, no symptoms)
    I: Symptomatic infectious
    R: Recovered, immune
    E → E while d_i^E < T_inc
    E infectious when d_i^E ≥ (T_inc − T_pre)
    E → I when d_i^E ≥ T_inc (deterministic)
    I → R when d_i^I ≥ T_inf (deterministic)

    Transmission

    λ_i = (β / N) × Σ_j d_ij^(−α)

    P(S→E)_i = 1 − exp(−λ_i)

    COVID-19 dual-kernel:
    λ_i = (β / N) × Σ_j [ 0.5 · d_ij^(−0.8) + 0.5 · d_ij^(−2.5) ]

    Φ = presymptomatic E ∪ symptomatic I  |  β = transmission intensity  |  α = spatial decay  |  N = population size (normalisation)

    Transmission efficiency

    TE = Σ_{i ∈ C} c_i / |C|

    C = { i : state = R and seed = false }
    c_i = secondary infections caused by individual i

    Empirical mean secondary infections over completed non-seed cases. Not an estimated Rₜ — no generation interval assumption is used.

    Model stochasticity

    Transmission events are probabilistic (Bernoulli trials from the force of infection). Disease progression after infection is deterministic. Identical parameters may produce substantially different outbreak trajectories across runs.

    Stochastic transmission Deterministic progression Household clustering Power-law distance kernel No contact networks No mobility No vaccination

    Complete formulation, disease parameters, and notation: METHODS.md  |  Assumptions and limitations: ASSUMPTIONS.md
    Ref: Deardon et al. (2010), Statistica Sinica, 20(1), 239–261.

    The Exposed stage contains two phases: a latent period where transmission is not possible, and a presymptomatic period where infected individuals can spread disease before symptoms appear. Disease-specific timelines control exactly how long each phase lasts.

    What this model doesn't capture

    Honest limitations

    Contact networks Viral load variation Age-based susceptibility Indoor ventilation Vaccination status Immunity waning Asymptomatic spread Multiple strains

    This is a spatial individual-level model (ILM) for educational purposes. It captures spatial transmission dynamics but not the full complexity of real epidemics.
